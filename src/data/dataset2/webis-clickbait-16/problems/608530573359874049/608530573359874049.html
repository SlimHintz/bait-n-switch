Rugby World Cup 2015: everything you need to know in 100 seconds

An incredible 2.3 million tickets are expected to be sold for the Rugby World Cup - watch this video which looks at the key stats of the tournament with 100 days to go

By Joe Daunt, and Oliver Pickup

7:39AM BST 10 Jun 2015

Comments

The Rugby World Cup 2015 is expected to sell around 2.3 million tickets and is taking place at 13 different match venues across the UK.

As we mark 100 days to go until the start of the tournament on September 18 - we look at some of the competition's key statistics.


The Webb Ellis Cup sits on the pitch with rugby balls showing the World Cup venues
New Zealand are strong favourites to win the World Cup, having been victorious twice before.

However, England will be feeling optimistic because of the home advantage.


Stuart Lancaster: England Rugby World Cup 2015 head coach

The final will be held at Twickenham on October 31.

Face value for some of those tickets are £715 but on some unofficial wesbites they are selling for a staggering £56,000.

Watch this video to learn more about the eighth Rugby World Cup, which is expected to bring in £32 million worth of revenue in food and drink alone.
