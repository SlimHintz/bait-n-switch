'Halt and Catch Fire' Episode 2: Mutiny in a man's world

855
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By AJ Marechal2015-06-08 06:03:21 UTC

In the second episode of Halt and Catch Fire’s new season — cheekily titled “New Coke” — Donna and Cameron must battle sexist investors, erratic coders and even a scheming hacker living in an unassuming suburban home. This, just to keep Mutiny afloat.

The pair’s online gaming company is growing at a rapid rate, leaving the women scrambling to build out their platforms and find new capital. Investors take meetings, but question the consumer demand for online gaming (only 10% of households had computers) and also question whether Donna and Cam can manage a company — they worry that since they are women, eventual motherhood will make them unreliable leaders, a concern that deeply offends both.

SEE ALSO: 'Halt and Catch Fire' Season 2 premiere: The birth of an Internet

Cameron continues to make unilateral hiring decisions that blindside Donna, including bringing Bosworth — former Cardiff exec and now ex-con — on board the fledgling company. And while Donna is constantly at her wits end with the coders and her often-rogue business partner, she and Cameron nevertheless balance one another, blending prudent decision-making with raw instinct and talent.

Gordon, meanwhile, has more free time than he knows what to do with, and is still struggling to shake a cocaine habit he picked up during his final stretch at Cardiff. Gordon sets up a computer station in his garage — his play-pen, of sorts — and, after several “What now?” moments, begins playing one of Mutiny’s games ...

And quickly spots a major tech issue.

Gordon cannot help himself. He races over to the Mutiny headquarters and lobs a fix for the timing issue in one of Mutiny’s flagship games, one that impresses the coders — Gordon, as a computer buff, still has the goods.

But Mutiny has a bigger problem on its hands — a coding prodigy named Tom has ripped off Parallax and hacked Mutiny in order to get the company’s attention. Cam tracks Tom down, only to find he’s nothing more than a normal guy with a desk job and a penchant for cooking.

Nevertheless, Cam sees the itch Tom has to dive into the tech world, where his talents can shine. She hires him — another unilateral move, and one that Donna says should be the last if Cameron wants to keep working with Donna.

Joe’s path to redemption continues at a snail’s pace as he gets to know his fiance’s father, a big-wig in the oil space. None of Joe’s job leads have panned out, and when Sara’s father offers Joe a job, he takes it, despite Sara’s reluctance. Whatever corner-office fantasy Joe had about the new gig was quickly put to rest when he discovers on Day One that his job is just data entry in a windowless room — a humiliating downgrade for arrogant, sharky Joe.

Sara confesses that her ex lost her father millions of dollars, and Joe recognizes that his future father-in-law is aggressively testing him. Joe bows his head to the task, and continues this bizarre ingratiation into Sara’s family.

Finally, there is Bosworth — fresh out of the pen, and still able to smile through a prank from the coders. But letters between him and Cameron while he was locked up reveal Bosworth’s isolation and loneliness; his entire life fell apart while at Cardiff, and he has almost no ties now to show for it, save Cam, his one friend in the outside world.

Now free, Cam begs Bosworth to work some of his CEO magic at Mutiny and get their business side in order. But Bosworth is still shaken by his loneliness, and admits he needs time to adjust to being out again — he just doesn’t know how much. With that, one of the people broken by Joe’s reign of manipulative terror at Cardiff, walks away from his next phase in life.
