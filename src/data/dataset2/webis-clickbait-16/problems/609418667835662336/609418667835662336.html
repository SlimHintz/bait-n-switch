Take a peek inside Apple's swanky new Upper East Side store in NYC

1.8k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Chris Perkins2015-06-12 17:41:35 UTC

While areas of New York City's Upper East Side have been infiltrated by yuccies over the past few years, the neighborhood is still one of the city's ritziest.

So naturally, the new Apple Store opening there is suitably fancy.

The brick-and-mortar store, located at the corner of East 74th Street and Madison Avenue, will open to the public on Saturday at 9 a.m. ET. The building was the former home of the U.S. Mortgage & Trust Bank in the 1920s, and Apple designed the space to capture the elegant spirit of that age.

See also: Fake Apple Store employees diss Apple, promote Microsoft products

Apple will even use the original bank vault in the basement for a VIP showroom, which makes sense; it's not hard to imagine that's where the $17,000 Apple Watch Editions will sit.

The company has a something of a tendency to integrate its retail stores into historic areas in major cities, like its stores at Grand Central Terminal in New York City and across from the Paris Opera House. It doesn't try to stand out, like the awning of a Starbucks or McDonalds might.

Unlike some of its other New York retail stores, the Upper East Side location is catering more to locals and businesses instead of tourists. The iconic glass cube at 57th Street can tend to get extremely busy with super-long lines, so a neighborhood-focused store takes some of the pressure off — not to mention trying to appease nearby residents who appreciate the relative quiet of the neighborhood.

Despite Apple's best efforts, though, some locals are in vocal opposition to the store. Herbert Feinberg, an 88-year-old retired winemaker who lives around the corner from the new Upper East Side store, filed a suit against Apple claiming the store will lower property values in the area. A local group, Saving the 74th Street Residential Neighborhood, circulated a petition against the store; it has garnered more than 400 signatures.

You can decide for yourself starting Saturday if Apple is ruining the neighborhood — or save yourself the trip and check out the photos below.
