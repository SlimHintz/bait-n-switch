      It was not a pretty start to the World Cup for the U.S. women. Australia looked like the better side in their first match and at many points looked poised to take a lead. Whether it was nerves, a lack of communication–or just that the Australians were quicker and able to penetrate–it did not paint an optimistic forecast for the rest of the tournament. 

      But that changed quickly. Christen Press scored to put the Americans up 2-1, and Megan Rapinoe scored her second of the game to cement a 3-1 victory. 

      The good news for the Americans: It wasn’t pretty for the Swedes, either. In one of the World Cup’s most surprising finishes to date, Nigeria–the only team not ranked in FIFA’s top-10 in Group D–played Sweden to a thrilling 3-3 draw. 

      The big question for this game: Does former U.S. coach Pia Sundhage, now Sweden’s coach, have the inside knowledge to send the U.S. to defeat? As WSJ’s Matthew Futterman, on the scene in Winnipeg wrote, this will be a test of American power vs. Swedish engineering. 

      Stay tuned here all game for the action as we’ll bring you play-by-play, analysis, jokes and most importantly, critiques of the Americans socks. (They wore the once-chic bright neon yellow socks, which we covered heavily in Monday’s live blog.)

      It will be interesting to watch whether this is a change that sticks for the rest of the tournament. Or, with an older player like Wambach, it could be tactful to give her a full half–or even a full game–of rest so she’s fresh for a more limited period on the pitch.

      All of this also dances around the elephant in the room: Alex Morgan’s health. She’s the star attacker, but nursing a knee injury and only came on in the 78th minute of the 3-1 win, when the U.S. was already up two scores. But she appeared ready to go in when it was 1-1. Will she play a bigger role today? And if not now, will she remain a super sub for the rest of the tournament? It’s a role she’s used to, coming off the bench in five of six games in the 2011 World Cup.

      After much hype, this game ends 0-0. 

      As Matthew said earlier, Sweden played into its defense. They kept eight back at times as they battled the Americans who put in a deep combination of strikers, subbing in Alex Morgan, Abby Wambach and Amy Rodriguez to try and vitalize the offense. 

      The Americans may have had more looks in the box, but Sweden had the best chance: A screamer off a corner kick that was saved not by Hope Solo, who played a magical game against Australia, but instead Klingenberg and her head on the goal line. She headed the attempt into the crossbar, saving the Americans from going down 1-0. 

      What happened in the game? There were no yellow socks for the Americans. There was corner kick after corner kick for both teams, but neither team was able to capitalize. The winners of this game are the back fours for both teams–perhaps a relief to both teams after the units looked shaky in their respective opening games. 

      With that, the Swedes gain their second point in Group D after also tying Nigeria. Meanwhile, the Americans gain another point and get to four after beating Australia, who beat Nigeria for their first three points. 

      Next for the Americans: Nigeria, an athletic team that gave Sweden fits on defense.
