Up close and personal! GoPro captures herd of wild rhinos checking out camera and eating grass in natural habitat

  • The herd of curious rhinos approach the GoPro and sniff it in turn
  • One nudges the camera with its nose before eating in front of it
  • The footage was shot in 2014 but has only recently emerged online

ByJake Polden For Mailonline

Published: 13:12 GMT, 10 June 2015 | Updated: 15:49 GMT, 10 June 2015

27 shares

View
comments

A herd of wild rhinos were captured in their natural habitat in some remarkable close-up footage recorded on a GoPro.

The video was shot by a visitor to the Kruger National Park in South Africa and shows the curious herd approaching the camera for a sniff.

After checking it out, the lead rhino wanders off and allows room for another to come in and have a look.

South Africa has the largest population of rhinos in the world, but poaching continues to be a problem

The rhinos take it in turn to approach and sniff the GoPro camera before one begins eating in front of it

The second rhino also goes right up to the camera and sniffs it before nudging it slightly with its nose.

It then decides to start eating in front of it and the camera captures the large mammal chewing on some grass.
