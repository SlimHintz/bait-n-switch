Tom Hanks is outdated, says son Chet

24-year-old rapper son Chester Hanks says his actor father's warnings about using social media are views of 'the old generation'

Tom Hanks with his son Chester Photo: Rex

By Martin Chilton, Culture Editor online

9:24AM BST 10 Jun 2015

Follow

Chester Hanks has dismissed concerns about his use of social media from his 58-year-old father Tom as the outdated views of "the old generation".

Tom Hanks, who has won two Oscars, six Emmys and four Golden Globes during his acting career, had reportedly told his 24-year-old son to "steer clear of social media sites" after the rapper (who likes to be known as Chet Haze) came under attack for using the N-word on Instagram.

Chester Marlon Hanks, whose acting career appropriately includes a role in the film Bratz, was interviewed in Santa Monica, California, by celebrity gossip website TMZ and revealed that his father, and mother Rita Wilson, had urged him to step away from social media. He told TMZ: "My parents are just making sure I'm good, they don't really tell me what to do any more because I'm grown [up], they just be making sure I'm happy, I'm safe. That's all... My parents they be telling me to just stay off social media period... They would be like, 'Don't be on Instagram so much' but they're the old generation, they don't get it. This is the new generation. We do things differently now. If you don't get it, you're not going to get it."

He also said that the N-word is used to express "camaraderie and love" in the hip-hop community.

Chet, who also starred in Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull (2008) and Project X (2012), is the older brother of Truman Theodore Hanks and younger half-brother of Dexter star Colin Hanks and Elizabeth Hanks.

Earlier this week, Sussex Police revealed that they want to question Chester Hanks after reports that £1,200 worth of damage was caused at the Arora International Hotel in Crawley, near Gatwick Airport.
