Babb vs Baggio: the six-a-side tournament that time forgot

The 1997 Amsterdam Sony Sixes were an odd experiment

Joy of Six: The Amsterdam Arena plays host to the 1997 tournament 

By Adam Hurrey

3:10PM BST 12 Jun 2015

Follow

Comments

In many ways, football was much simpler in the 1990s: the only way to immerse yourself in transfer nonsense was to force your parents to remortgage their house after spending £1 a minute ringing ClubCall to find out who your big SERIE A GOAL ACE summer signing was going to be, Football Focus was still at the cutting edge of top-flight news, and the idea of a World Cup in Qatar was like something out of a Sega MegaDrive game.

Nevertheless, two decades before knackered players were being dragged around the globe on post-season tours and pre-season tours, one Dutch footballing pioneer with Guilder signs in their eyes decided it was a great idea to bring four of Europe's biggest clubs together for a six-a-side tournament in Amsterdam in January 1997, halfway through the regular season.

Ring any bells? Amid the surprisingly rich history of indoor football, you still have to dig quite deep to find any trace of the 1997 Euro Sixes at the newly-opened Amsterdam Arena. The two-day tournament, devised by Johan Cruyff, pitted together AC Milan, Rangers, Liverpool and hosts Ajax over a round-robin series of games divided into four eight-minute quarters.

More than 21,000 fans turned up under the stadium's state-of-the-art retractable roof to witness this extra-curricular curiosity, which was sponsored by Sony MiniDisc - another concept that was clearly way ahead of its time - just to rubberstamp the vintage feel in retrospect.

• 10 football experiments that were doomed to failure

The competition received official UEFA approval, which was handy, and global television rights were quickly snapped up. A spokesman gave the whole thing the big sell:

Everything about the games here will be based on technical ability. Each team will have 11 players stripped for every game. Six of them will play and five will be substitutes and the matches will last for four eight-minute spells with substitutions being allowed at any time.

The games themselves served as a sobering experience for the two British representatives. First, Rangers were thumped 6-2 by Ajax - including goals from Patrick Kluivert, Jari Litmanen and Marc Overmars - before Liverpool took to the unsteady Arena turf to face AC Milan. Roy Evans' 2-2-1 formation, with Phil Babb marshalling the defence and Robbie Fowler on the prowl up front, looked solid enough.

All of the games will be played, obviously, on a smaller pitch than usual, but we are still working on the final specifications with the technical advisers.

Milan eventually ran out 5-0 winners, with Liverpool barely stringing together two passes. This was not the dream, even if Jamie Carragher has only find memories of it all:

It was brilliant! Got Edgar David's shirt & a night out in Amsterdam!! https://t.co/AC8CxZsuG4

— Jamie Carragher (@Carra23) June 13, 2015

Ajax and Milan then faced each other - resulting in a respectably sensible 2-2 draw - before Liverpool's misery was compounded by a 7-2 defeat to Paul Gascoigne's Rangers. In the final, Milan again ran riot, this time taking the hosts apart 6-2, thanks to an Overmars own goal and Baggio - who would be named player of the tournament - scoring directly from a corner.

The wisdom of fielding full-strength lineups was questioned, however, when Gascoigne picked up an ankle injury in a collision with Ajax goalkeeper Fred Grim. Leaving the stadium on crutches, Gascoigne was forced to miss England's vital World Cup qualifier against Italy at Wembley - Matt Le Tissier took his place, to little acclaim, and England lost 1-0.

Despite the lucrative sums on offer - each club were given a cool £120,000 for taking part - and the promise of Bayern Munich, Juventus and Real Madrid attending the following year's tournament, the Sony Euro Sixes were (to the internet's knowledge, at least) never to be repeated.
