6 low-sweat exercises to beat the summer heat

2.0k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Kathleen Wong2015-06-13 11:05:47 UTC

It's easy to forgo a quick workout during your lunch break, between classes or before dinner dates in order to stay presentable.

See also: 13 Plane Confessions From the People Running Your Flight

Well, no more excuses because here is a simple — but not easy — workout that'll keep the sweat factor low and the efficiency high. The moves are low on cardio and focus mainly on body weight, so sweating is a minimum.

Having been in fitness for over nine years, Prince Brathwaite, founder of boot camp Trooper Fitness created a workout routine that'll take about 30 minutes, meaning you should repeat the circuit three to five times.

It's the perfect routine to tackle during the warm sweat-inducing months of summer, or you know, for any other excuse you have in the book.

1. Overhead squats


Start the routine with 20 overhead squats, keeping arms up in the air. Make sure your back is straight and your weight is on your heels and the balls of your feet.

2. Step-ups


Follow the squats with 20 basic step-ups, alternating feet and keeping your hands at your hips.

3. Basic tricep dips


Perform 20 tricep dips, resting on your heels and lowering yourself your elbows to a 90-degree angle.

4. Dive bombers


Next, channel your inner yogi by forming an inverted V with your body. Lower your shoulders and glide forward so that your head and chest are now towards the ceiling. Reverse the push-up by lifting your hips back up.

5. Lifting arm plank


This move will tighten your core and improve your balance. Get in normal plank position with your hands on the ground and then alternative lifting arms. Do this for one minute.

6. Twisting kick-through planks


In plank position with your arms shoulder-width apart, lift one leg and bring it under your body until you can touch the tips of your extended opposite hand. Do 10 of these, alternating legs.
