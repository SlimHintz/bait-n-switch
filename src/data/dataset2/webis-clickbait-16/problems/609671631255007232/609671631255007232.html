Suspect in Dallas Police Department attack confirmed dead after standoff

3.4k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Jonathan Ellis2015-06-13 10:37:19 UTC

UPDATED: 4:40 p.m. ET

In an apparent planned attack on the Dallas Police Department, a gunman opened fire on police headquarters early Saturday morning, fled in an armored vehicle, and entered into a standoff with officers at a nearby Jack in the Box parking lot, police said.

The police department confirmed on Saturday afternoon that the suspect was dead, following the standoff.

#Breaking We can now confirm that the susp in the van is deceased but unable to confirm ID pending Med Ex. identification #DallasPDShooting

— Dallas Police Depart (@DallasPD) June 13, 2015

At a news conference Saturday morning, Dallas Police Chief David O. Brown said officers had shot the suspect through the window of the vehicle in the parking lot, but it took several hours to confirm his death for fear that the car was rigged with explosives.

The suspect's vehicle subsequently caught fire, with leftover ammunition going off inside the van, as a result of an "intentional detonation" from the authorities, according to a tweet from the police department's Twitter account.

Meanwhile, police found two explosive devices left outside their headquarters, both of which were detonated by explosives robots. One of the devices had been left under a police truck.

This is the first time DPD HQ has been under attack.

— Dallas Police Depart (@DallasPD) June 13, 2015

Despite many shots being fired, no officers were hurt in the attack.

"We literally dodged bullets," Brown said. "Thankfully, we're here talking about [it]; all officers are okay."

"We barely survived the intent of this suspect.”

Brown did not identify the suspect, but said that in a rambling, "high-pitched" five-minute call to the department's 911 line, the suspect blamed police for taking his child and accusing him of being a terrorist.

While police initially thought there were multiple gunmen, it now appears to have been the work of one person, Brown said. He added that the suspect appeared to be “shooting from different angles, different positions making witnesses think it was multiple suspects.”

#BREAKING inside @Dallaspd HQ riddled with bullet holes #DallasPDShooting pic.twitter.com/jIZqd78di9

— Maj. Max Geron (@MaxDPD) June 13, 2015

The shootout

The suspect was believed to be in an armored van when the shooting began around 12:30 a.m. local time. In this video, it appears an officer checks the van with a flashlight, then flees, and then a burst of gunfire erupts as squad cars position themselves nearby:

Dallas police officer running for his life after realizing the threat was the armored van! pic.twitter.com/TRjePXFZJS

— Germanito006 (@germanito006) June 13, 2015

This video shows the van ramming a police cruiser, then backing up and speeding away amid ensuing gunfire as police gave chase:

Armored van lured police, fired upon, and rammed police pic.twitter.com/luf2U1SMje

— Madhu (@madwho12) June 13, 2015

Police released images of some of the damage at their headquarters:

Here are some pictures of the damage at HQ. pic.twitter.com/ic18axSTPO

— Dallas Police Depart (@DallasPD) June 13, 2015

Squad cars were also hit by gunfire but no officers were injured in the shootout, the department said. Officers returned fire, Chief David said. Police said they could not confirm any motive.

Picture of squad car that was shot at. No officers were injured. pic.twitter.com/vfT2nCiJzP

— Dallas Police Depart (@DallasPD) June 13, 2015

At a pre-dawn briefing, Brown said witnesses reported seeing multiple gunmen. But later in the day, he said officials now believed there was only one, the man who was later in the standoff at the Jack-in-the-Box parking lot. After suspect called 911 and left a rambling message, police returned the call, speaking to and trying to negotiate with the suspect.

At least 20 police cars outside Dallas Police Headquarters after shooting pic.twitter.com/1RcK7dYvzR

— Ashley Pettit (@ashleypettit) June 13, 2015

The explosives

All suspicious packages outside the headquarters have now been cleared, police said. One of the two explosive devices apparently detonated as an explosives robot tried to move it. Other suspicious bags turned out to be trash.

This is where the Explosive Ordinance Robot attempted to move one of the bags. #DallasPDShooting pic.twitter.com/mgbgytBhWv

— Dallas Police Depart (@DallasPD) June 13, 2015

Police said at least one bag had "pipe bombs" inside.

#Breaking 2 explosive devices were found @DallasPD HQ - all suspected packages have now been cleared there. #DallasPDShooting

— Dallas Police Depart (@DallasPD) June 13, 2015

The explosive ordnance disposal robot apparently suffered some damage in the operation.

#Breaking Our EOD robot took a hit from the initial explosion but is still functional and in use #DallasPDShooting

— Dallas Police Depart (@DallasPD) June 13, 2015

The standoff

After the shooting, the suspect drove the van south to a Jack in the Box parking lot in nearby Hutchins, Texas.

The suspect identified himself to police as James Boulware, the Dallas PD said. It's not known if the suspect is really Boulware, but CNN reported that Boulware was previously known to police for making threats. At a press conference, Brown described Boulware as "a white male, 5'10", 200 pounds."

After an exchange of gunfire in the parking lot, a SWAT team engaged in negotiations with the suspect or suspects by cellphone. They have refused to leave their vehicle, police said, so the SWAT team "disabled" the vehicle with a gunshot.

Dallas SWAT disabled the suspect vehicle with a .50 cal rifle. Standoff is ongoing #DallasPDShooting

— Dallas Police Depart (@DallasPD) June 13, 2015

Brown said that the suspect "has told our negotiators that we took his child and we accused him of being a terrorist, and that he's going to blow us up," before cutting off negotiations.

No officers have been injured in the parking lot standoff, Brown said.
