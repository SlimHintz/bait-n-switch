    • 5 Highlights of Japan’s Revised GDP Report

    Japan’s economy grew an annualized 3.9% in the first quarter of 2015, according to revised data released Monday. It’s a surprisingly strong figure for a country that has struggled to recover from a increase in the national sales tax last year. What’s driving the better figures—and can the momentum continue? Here are five highlights from the report.

    7 Jun 2015 10:46pm
    By
    WSJ Staff

    • 1 Surprise Upward Revision

      Share on Twitter

      The initial reading for first-quarter growth was an annualized rate of 2.4%. Economists had expected a modest upward revision, but the extent of the revision—to 3.9%—was a surprise.

    • 2 Business Investment Drives the Revision

      Share on Twitter

      The biggest lift to growth came from business investment, which is now estimated to have grown an annualized 11% over the previous quarter instead of 1.4%. Export-oriented manufacturing industries have been boosting investment in areas such as auto parts. In the first quarter, capital spending also picked up at hitherto slumping service industries, such as retailers and utilities, government officials said.

    • 3 Exports Are Boosting the Economy

      Share on Twitter

      Exports grew an annualized 9.9%, according to the latest data, unchanged from the earlier estimate. Until recently, the fall in the yen that began in late 2012 seemed to be having little impact on Japanese exports, in part because so many of the nation’s manufacturers had already moved production overseas. Now the data are beginning to show an uptick.

    • 4 Domestic Consumption Is Still Weak

      Share on Twitter

      When the national sales tax rose to 8% from 5% in April 2014, it chilled consumers’ willingness to spend, and their spirits have yet to recover. Household spending grew an annualized 1.5% in the first quarter—a figure unchanged by the revision. And in April, according to a separate government report, household spending fell 1.3% from a year earlier.

    • 5 Inventory Buildup Is a Concern

      Share on Twitter

      Many economists expect slower growth in the second quarter of 2015, and they point to an inventory buildup as a concern. Growth in business inventories accounted for slightly more than half of the 3.9% annualized growth rate. Economists on average expect GDP growth to slow to 1.7% in the second quarter and 2.0% in the third quarter, according to the Japan Center for Economic Research.
