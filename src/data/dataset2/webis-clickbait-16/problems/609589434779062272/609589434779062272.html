5 hilarious fathers on Twitter who tell more than 'dad jokes'

1.6k
Shares
Share
Tweet

What's This?
By Eli Epstein2015-06-12 19:58:13 UTC

Dads are in.

Across media, entertainment and pop culture, there's a growing fascination with fathers and all the trappings of fatherhood, from the dad joke renaissance taking place on social media to the "Dad Bod" trend sweeping the nation.

See also: Terrible Dad Humor to Make Your Kids Roll Their Eyes

The best thing about dad humor? You don't need to be one to chuckle (though the jokes may resonate more if you just spent an hour cleaning up toddler poop).

For a good laugh, and maybe some empathy, check out these nine hysterical dads on Twitter.

1. @ReasonsMySonCry

Our NYE tradition is to pick an youtube clip of a ball drop, pretend it's live & still get the boys to bed by 9. Tonight we'll ring in 1991.

— ReasonsMySonIsCrying (@ReasonsMySonCry) December 31, 2014

Parents know that children — especially toddlers — cry for myriad reasons.

Cheerio on the floor turns into a life-changing cataclysm? Tears. Wall-E Blu-ray not in its usual spot? Temper tantrum.

The ReasonsMySonIsCrying account features both parenting quips from the tweeter and contributions from his legion of bewildered parent followers who sometimes can't help but chuckle at the outrageous reasons their children are sobbing (You'll only play with the iPhone and not the Blackberry, really?).

Elsewhere, his humor can be found here.

"He touched a water chestnut." - Julie in California pic.twitter.com/BphjM3tya7

— ReasonsMySonIsCrying (@ReasonsMySonCry) May 2, 2015

2. @lunchboxdad

My @playmobil Ruby Pirate Super 4 #bento lunch for my kids: http://t.co/rY8JNPSVFB #StreamTeam pic.twitter.com/1NvJjAovJm

— Beau Coffron (@lunchboxdad) May 26, 2015

Blogger Beau Coffron, aka the Lunchbox Dad, is a triple threat on Twitter — advocate, humorist and lunch designer.

That's right — in addition to wisecracks about his children's adolescence, and support for causes like childhood obesity and seatbelt use, Coffron also designs some of the coolest school lunches you've ever seen, like this Star Wars-themed meal for MayThe4th.

His witty banter is what got him on this list, however. C'mon, what parents can't relate to Frozen overload?

Driving for a school field trip today. According to the kids, car music choices are Frozen, Frozen, or Frozen. #parenting

— Beau Coffron (@lunchboxdad) April 24, 2015

3. @FatherWithTwins

Hey, if you don't like my 5yo, that's fine, but you don't have to give him the Donatello plate. That's just rude.

— Father With Twins (@FatherWithTwins) May 31, 2015

Ahhh, the pleasures of parenting — feigning interest in inane shows, becoming a human napkin, absorbing all the new smells and sounds that pervade your household.

It all might drive you mad if you don't counteract it with positivity and some much-needed humor.

FatherWithTwins takes his twins' barrage of questions in stride. One thing he teaches other parents: Don't let your kids know that you can Google ANYTHING on a smartphone.

The touch tank lady got frustrated when my 5yo asked for the 3rd time what kind of fish it was. Hang on, he's gonna ask about 50 more times

— Father With Twins (@FatherWithTwins) May 1, 2015

4. @byclintedwards

Co-worker: Your wife is a vegetarian? Do your kids eat meat? Me: If it's shaped like a dinosaur.

— No Idea: Daddy Blog (@byclintedwards) June 1, 2015

The No Idea: Daddy Blog by Clint Edwards brings humor to some of the more unsavory parts of childcare that non-parents tend to forget about — like having to nuke your dining room after your kids eat there or finding that diaper you left in the car two days ago.

But, as Clint demonstrates, it's always important to remember that your tykes — no matter how many liquids they've spilled — are the cutest and best things that ever happened to you.

Because of my 5-year-old I've had to learn how to speak princess, puppy, and unicorn. None of these languages will help me professionally.

— No Idea: Daddy Blog (@byclintedwards) May 7, 2015

5. @whithonea

Summer vacation can't come fast enough. I'm sick of waiting all day for the kids to unload the dishwasher.

— Whit Honea (@whithonea) June 1, 2015

Author of The Parents' Phrase Book, Whit Honea has plenty of quirky (and honest) parenting observations to share on Twitter.

They're especially resonant if you're having one of those days as a parent where everything is going wrong. They're late, you're late, the house is a mess — you get the point.

Aside from Twitter, Honea also runs @dads4change, an account that highlights fathers and their families who champion social-good initiatives.

Hand sanitizer is my favorite condiment.

— Whit Honea (@whithonea) January 20, 2015
