Prince Harry meets Lady Gaga at Royal Albert Hall

Prince Harry and Lady Gaga were both supporting the charity WellChild at a gala concert

By Gordon Rayner, Chief Reporter, video source APTN

9:15PM BST 08 Jun 2015

Follow

Prince Harry met pop royalty in the form of Lady Gaga tonight when they joined forces to raise money for the WellChild charity at a Royal Albert Hall gala concert.

While the 30-year-old Prince went for a casual look in a navy suit and open-necked white shirt, Lady Gaga chose a sequinned dress by Brandon Maxwell with diamante eyebrows and huge false eyelashes.

Lady Gaga and Tony Bennett have recorded an album of duets (The Picture Library)

By Lady Gaga's usual standards, it was a relatively understated look, particularly when compared to the red latex outfit she wore when the met the Queen after the Royal Variety Performance in 2009.

The Prince was greeted by the Lord Lieutenant of London Ken Olisa, the President of the Royal Albert Hall Peta Travis and the WellChild chief executive Colin Dyer.

Prince Harry arrives at the Royal Albert Hall (Getty Images)

He was also introduced to the American singer Tony Bennett who has collaborated with Lady Gaga on a new album.

Gaga told Harry: "We think you are fantastic" as the royal thanked them for supporting the charity.

Discussing how they collaborated on their new jazz album Cheek To Cheek, Bennett, 88, said: "I heard her personally and said we have to get together."

Harry told him: "I can't believe you have the energy, I don't know how you do it."

Gaga, 29, replied: "He runs circles around me. Wait until we get on stage."

Then Bennett said to Harry about his late mother Princess Diana: "Your mum was a good friend of mine. I did many benefits with her."

Harry replied: "That's fantastic."

The Prince, who has been patron of WellChild since 2007, later met families who have benefited from its support as well as staff, nurses and donors to the charity at a reception before the event.

He then headed to the Royal Box to watch Gaga, 29, perform alongside Bennett, 88, as they celebrate their new Jazz album Cheek To Cheek.

The Prince entertains the entertainers (The Picture Library)

WellChild chief executive Colin Dyer said: "This is such a big night for the charity."

WellChild funds nurses who provide practical and emotional support to children in their own homes and carries out improvements and modifications to their homes and gardens, as well as funding research into childhood illnesses.
