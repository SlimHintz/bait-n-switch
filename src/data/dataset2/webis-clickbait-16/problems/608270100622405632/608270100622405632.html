Reinventing Russian roulette: Shocking moment player is zapped with blast of electricity while taking part in a bizarre new version of deadly game

  • New game is taking the Russian city of Perm by storm after being invented by a local mixed martial arts fighter
  • Valeriy Eschenko invented the game after a rare nerve condition called Guillain-Barré left him paralysed
  • Unable to fight and recovering in hospital, Mr Eschenko decided to create a new game which he could referee 
  • It requires players to replace one bullet chamber with an electric shock machine then spin the weapon's barrel
  • The winner is the person who manages to pull the trigger most frequently without receiving an electric shock  

ByJohn Hall for MailOnline

This is the moment a man playing a bizarre new version of Russian roulette gets zapped with an electric shock in the country that gave the deadly game its name.

The game, which is becoming increasingly popular in the Russian city of Perm, requires participants to replace one of the bullet chambers in a gun with a small electric shock mechanism.

The players then spin the barrel and put the gun to their head - not knowing whether it has landed on an empty chamber or the one containing the electric shock - before pulling the trigger.

The new version of Russian Roulette is the brainchild of former mixed martial arts fighter Valeriy Eschenko, who came up with the idea while bored in hospital after being forced to retire when a rare nerve condition called Guillain-Barré syndrome put him in a coma and left him paralysed.

After coming up with the idea to occupy his thoughts and time in hospital, Mr Eschenko patented the game's intellectual property, and got proper equipment made.

It is now becoming increasingly popular in the former fighter's home city of Perm.

'I was a professional mixed martial arts fighter but suddenly I found myself in a coma,' he said.

'It was connected to the constant sparring and fights; all my body parts were paralysed. I couldn't even move my fingers,' Mr Eschenko added.

While receiving treatment in hospital, the former fighter said he spent hours contemplating his purpose in life and trying to decide what to do for a living now that he'd been forced to retire. 

'Finally the idea of this 'Russian roulette game' crystallized. This was a new extreme but, at the same time, safe game,' he said.

Having come up with the rules of the game and designing special guns, Mr Eschenko now hosts tournaments in his home city twice a month. Participants are each charged £3.20 to take part.

The pneumatic gas guns used in the game are made in China and any player who receives a shock from them is considered to have been 'killed'.

However, players can also 'bluff' a shot by trying to disguise the fact they have received a shock.

Mr Eschenko usually acts as the referee and contests can sometimes be decided on who reacts the best to having the electric shock coursing through their body.

One unnamed player said: 'It absolutely is worth the money. I have never had so many emotions. 

'When you are waiting for this moment of shot and are not sure if you are going to be hit or not - you have just unreal feelings,'  he added.

Mr Eschenko says the best part about the game is that the player is completely in charge of their own destiny.

'You spin the barrel yourself, so you decide your destiny yourself - everything is in your hands and depends on you only,' he said.

'If the sound of the gun is weak it means no one is killed,' he added.

'If the sound is louder, the player starts to feel something really unpleasant - strong pain and the muscles start twitching - it means the player is killed,' Mr Eschenko went on to say.
