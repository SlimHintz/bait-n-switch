How America has responded to the many faces of Hillary Clinton

  • Jun. 13, 2015, 10:57 AM

ReutersFormer U.S. Secretary of State Hillary Clinton and Goldman Sachs Group Inc. Chairman and Chief Executive Officer Lloyd Blankfein listen to speakers during the plenary session titled "Equality for Girls and Women: 2034 Instead of 2134?" at the Clinton Global Initiative 2014

With Hillary Clinton making her first big speech at a campaign event since announcing her run for the White House, she will seek once again on Saturday to reshape her image, one that has evolved tremendously over the 23 years she’s been a public figure.

Here’s a look at the most memorable chapters of her public life and how America responded to each iteration of Hillary.

1993-1998: First-Lady-In-Chief

Vocativ

When Bill Clinton took office, Hillary became the first First Lady to have a postgrad degree and a career. Bill promised Americans would get “two for the price of one” as Hillary was heavily involved in presidential affairs.

She vetted new positions in Bill’s administration, led the Task Force on National Health Care Reform, and was instrumental to the passage of health, family and women’s rights policies. Some people thought it was inappropriate for the First Lady to be so involved in presidential matters.

During the Whitewater investigation, her favorability numbers plunged to 43 percent.

1998: Stand-By-Your-Man Hillary

Vocativ

While Bill was being investigated for having a sexual affair with his intern Monica Lewinsky, Hillary issued a statement reaffirming her commitment to Bill, saying in an interview on NBC’s Today show “The great story here for anybody willing to find it and write about it and explain it is this vast right-wing conspiracy that has been conspiring against my husband since the day he announced for president.”

Her support for her husband resonated with Americans: Her favorability ratings reached 67 percent, the peak of her poll numbers over the two decades.

1999: Carpetbagger Clinton

Vocativ

When Hillary decided to run for a New York senate seat, she and Bill, who had deep roots in Arkansas, bought a house just north of New York City, in Chappaqua. Many opponents painted her as a carpetbagger who had no right participating in New York politics.

Her campaign targeted upstate Republican-heavy areas of the state, and focused on job creation. Clinton won with 55 percent of the vote.

2001-2007: Senator Clinton

Vocativ

As a Senator, Clinton built alliances with many Republicans. She kept a low profile at first, but by the end, had become one of her party’s leaders in the Senate.

After the Sept. 11 attacks, she supported the Patriot Act and the wars in Afghanistan and Iraq—decisions that would later come back to haunt her initial campaign for president.

2007-2008: Uncool Candidate Clinton

Vocativ

In 2007, Clinton became the first woman to be nominated by a major party for the presidency. But on June 7, 2008, after Barrack Obama earned enough delegates to become the presumptive nominee, she ended her run and endorsed Obama. Pundits blamed the loss on a combination of sexism and her inability to compete with Obama’s youth appeal.

2008-2012: Madame Secretary 

Vocativ

Clinton accepted Obama’s offer to become his Secretary of State. As Secretary, her approval rating reached 65 percent–one of her highest showings in the polls over the 23 years.

She was the most-traveled Secretary of State in American history, but there were black marks too: In 2012, the U.S. ambassador and three other Americans were killed in an attack on the diplomatic post in Benghazi, Libya. (The subsequent hit to her poll ratings didn’t come until after she had retired from the State Department.)

2012: Tumblr-y Clinton

Vocativ

A photo of Clinton sitting aboard a military C-17 Globemasster III plane, wearing sunglasses and staring at her Blackberry went viral and evolved into the Tumblr page Texts From Hillary.

The meme helped Clinton connect with a younger, connected demographic—a trend that has continued as she started tweeting in 2013.

Now: Champion-Of-The-Underdog Hillary

Vocativ

Hillary has continued to try to build her hip-factor, setting up her campaign headquarters in Brooklyn, starting an Instagram account, and selling products on her site that are ironic and have feminist messages.

When she makes her speech on New York City’s Roosevelt’s Island on Saturday, she is expected to set the tone for her campaign for the presidency—a fighter for the working class.
