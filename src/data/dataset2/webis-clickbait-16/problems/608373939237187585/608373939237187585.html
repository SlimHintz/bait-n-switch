Former Yahoo exec joins Snapdeal, a billion-dollar Indian marketplace

703

By JP Mangalindan2015-06-09 20:38:36 UTC

Former Yahoo executive Anand Chandrasekaran is leaving India's largest mobile operator Bharti Airtel after just 13 months to head product for Snapdeal, Mashable has learned from sources familiar with the matter.

Chandrasekaran, whose last day with Bharti Airtel is Wednesday, confirmed his departure from the business to Re/code in May but did not specify then where exactly he was headed.

See also: Twitter Arrives on TV in India

His move is significant given the companies involved and their positions in India, which was the world's best-performing emerging market last year, followed by the Philippines and Indonesia.

With more than 300 million customers in 20 countries across Asia and Africa, Bharti Airtel is the largest mobile carrier in India and the fourth largest in the world. The company drew fire from local activists in India this April over its Airtel Zero plaform, which lets users access data within select mobile apps for free â€” an incident that contributed to a backlash over Internet.org, the Facebook-backed service that aims to bring people in developing countries online.

The New Delhi-based Snapdeal has quickly become one of India's largest consumer Internet companies in the five years since it was founded by Kunal Bahl and Rohit Bansal. The company most recently raised $627 million last fall at a $1 billion-plus valuation, making it a so-called unicorn and joining a crowded group of tech startups with stratospheric valuations, including Snapchat, Uber, and Airbnb.

Prior to Bharti Airtel, Chandrasekaran worked at Yahoo for about three years as senior director of search and mobile products, according to his LinkedIn profile, where he helped lead the tech giant's global search business in areas like local search (obviously), content partnerships and the Maps platform.
