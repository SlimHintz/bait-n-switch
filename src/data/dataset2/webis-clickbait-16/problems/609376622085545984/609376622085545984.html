Behind the scenes of the largest annual meeting of global warming contrarians

1.9k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Dustin Drankoski2015-06-12 14:33:17 UTC

On Thursday, a group of people gathered in Washington D.C. to kick off one hell of a hootenanny. The two-day "International Climate Change Conference" brought together all manner of mainly old white guys to talk about how continued fossil fuel use could, in fact, save our planet and all of its impoverished people. None of the speakers said global warming is much of a problem at all, if it even exists.

See also: The global warming conference where skeptics think they're winning the debate

On Thursday's agenda was a keynote address by Oklahoma Republican Senator James Inhofe, who chairs the Senate Environment Committee and believes global warming to be a "hoax," as well as a panel on climate science that featured an astrophysicist named Willie Soon.

Soon, who has long argued, in spite of evidence to the contrary, that the sun is the main cause of climate change, is under investigation by several institutions for failing to disclose research funding from the oil and gas industry.

The keynote lunch was led by Rep. Lamar Smith (R-Texas), an ardent climate contrarian who chairs the House Science Committee. Smith recently slashed the climate research budgets of NASA and the National Science Foundation. For this action and more, Rep. Smith accepted the 2015 "Courage in Defense of Science" award.

The conference was held by the Heartland Institute, a free-market think tank headquartered in Chicago.
