'Have you burnt her?' How mother of girl with huge birthmark on her face is accused of hurting the daughter she loves... and wants to silence the strangers that assume the worst

  • Sophia Lonergan was diagnosed with Sturge-Weber Syndrome at 10-weeks-old
  • It left a huge birth mark covering 75 per cent of her face and half of her body 
  • Now at 23-months-old she can't sit, crawl, feed herself, speak more than three words and has seizures
  • Her mother Mandy Lonergan is now campaigning to raise awareness of the condition 
  • She has stated a Sauce Ya Face campaign in order to appear on The Ellen DeGeneres Show

BySarah Carty for Daily Mail Australia

Published: 00:04 GMT, 10 June 2015 | Updated: 08:43 GMT, 10 June 2015

When new mum Mandy Lonergan first set eyes on her baby daughter her initial reaction was: 'What's wrong with my baby?'

While her 38-year-old's husband Ryan whooped for joy in the delivery room, the 30-year-old mother from Ipswich in Queensland could tell from her daughter Sophia's bright red complexion that something just wasn't quite right.

Doctors believed the redness was a result of the birth but after Sophia began suffering seizures at ten-weeks-old while Mandy was breastfeeding, she was diagnosed with Type 1 Sturge-Weber Syndrome (SWS). The syndrome is believed to have caused the birthmark covering 75 per cent of her face and half of her body.

Sophia (pictured at 23-months-old)was diagnosed with Type 1 Sturge-Weber Syndrome (SWS) leaving a noticeable birthmark covering 75 per cent of her face

Along with the facial redness, the neurological condition causes epilepsy, glaucoma and mental delay - meaning Sophia has the development of an eight-month-old and she's nearly two.

The adorable girl, who is now 23-months-old, can't sit, crawl, feed herself and she has the red port-wine stain all over one side of her body. 

For the first few months of her life Sophia didn't recognise who her parents were, leaving it hard for Mandy to bond with her newborn baby.

And while it's been hard dealing with the fact that their precious daughter may never walk or talk, the uneducated comments from strangers about her appearance have been just as hard to stomach.

'I understand she has a bright red face and people are going to look and people are curious - especially children - we understand that,' Mandy told Daily Mail Australia.

'But it’s the naive and insensitive comments that just aren't necessary.'

Mandy has been asked a wide-range of questions about Sophia's red face, with one woman accusing her of burning her child.

‘People have asked me have I burnt her? Is she sunburned? What’s wrong with her face? What’s that on her face? Is that a rash?' she said.

However the gorgeous girl (pictured at two-and-a-half-weeks-old) was diagnosed with Type 1 Sturge-Weber Syndrome

'People have taken their children as if to get away from us, as if it’s contagious. It’s not bloody contagious.‘

Even a medical worker once thought Sophia was suffering from burn marks when she walked into the hospital for a check-up. 

She said Sophia's condition has opened up her eyes to a lot of things, including how judgmental and opinionated people can be, although she believes their comments are not meant to be mean.

Passersby have asked if Sophia is sunburned, if she has eczema and worst of all parents have diverted their children away from Sophia's direction, fearing her condition is contagious.

'I used to get very anxious and teary and I’d just go home but now I’m open for a dialogue if you want to talk about I’ll talk about it,' Mandy said.

'It’s not always meant to be hurtful but what they don’t realise is that it is and it’s not so much to me anymore but when Sophia’s a teenager - if she can comprehend what’s going on around her - she’ll struggle with people just saying stupid things like that.'

Sophia's mum said her condition has opened up her eyes to how judgmental people can really be 

Mandy said it is hurtful when people on the street made random comments about her daughter's appearance, however she believes they are just uneducated about Sophia's condition 

Mandy has started the Sauce Ya Face campaign, asking people to smear sauce on their faces and nominate three others to do the same. Sophia and Many are pictured here with family members

When Mandy was told that her daughter would always have to be on medication to treat her condition she said she asked her mother 'why me?'.

The couple had prepared themselves for the worst but when they heard the words come from the doctor's mouth she said they were devastated. 

'My heart broke but it wasn't for me because my life is okay, I’m okay but for her we don’t know what it’s going to mean for her life,' she said. 

'Without sounding ridiculous I’m a really nice human being, I care for everyone, I'm non judgmental, I am so sweet, I worry about everyone so then I thought Why me? Why did I get this journey and path?

'But as my mum reminded me, all those characteristics are why she came to me because she needs all of those things. 

'Although I didn't understand it at the start I have overcome all the fears of what if? 

'We didn't care about a birth mark that wasn't going to be an issue for our family. Then when she started having seizures it was life-changing in that instant.' 

The condition means that Sophia (pictured at two-months-old) has a birth mark covering 75 per cent of her face and half of her body 

The beautiful girl (pictured at three-and-a-half-weeks-old) also has epileptic episodes, glaucoma and is unable to walk, talk or crawl

However Sophia (pictured at ten-months-old) has defied doctors and has muttered the words Mum, Dad and the colour brown

While Mandy won't know the full extent of Sophia's condition until she is four-year-old, she has high hopes that she will walk and talk on her own one day.

Despite claiming she will never talk, Sophia has uttered the words mum, dad and randomly the colour brown. 

For now, Sophia has undergone two rounds of laser treatment on her facial birthmark as her mother said it can become hard, pebbly and overgrown if left untreated.  

'We're going to continue with that, she looks like a cheetah cub,' she said. 

The happy-go-lucky little girl is also on medication to regulate her seizures and uses drops for her glaucoma. 

Sophia's diagnosis has certainly changed Mandy's perspective and now she has started a social media campaign called Sauce Ya Face in the hope of appearing on The Ellen DeGeneres Show to raise awareness of the condition.

In a twist, a couple in Melbourne - who have a daughter with the same condition -  gave Mandy and her husband half of their one million Qantas air miles which they won in a competition.

The couple graciously handed it over to the Lonergans so they can attend the international Sturge-Weber Syndrome conference in three weeks time in the US.

Her parents Ryan and Mandy (pictured) were devastated when they first heard that their daughter may never walk or talk 

However they now want to raise awareness of her condition to stop the hateful comments 

The family are flying to the US in two-and-a-half weeks to attend the international Sturge-Weber Syndrome conference 

Deeply humbled by the generous prize, Mandy thought the best way to thank them would be to gain awareness of SWS and bring it to light on The Ellen Show.

The Sauce Ya Face campaign was born to get Ellen's attention, with people all over the web smearing sauce on their face and then nominating three others to do the same.

Mandy said that she is campaigning for Sophia's future in the hope that if she has a chance to grow older she will be able to walk down the street without people staring in her direction.

'We want her to be strong and fierce,' she said. 

We won’t know if she can comprehend the world around her until she’s older. 

'Let’s give her a good background so if she does get to be older and understand she knows that we fought for her and we believe in her and she’ll be okay.' 

While in the US, Mandy hopes to appear on The Ellen Show to raise awareness for the condition 

Mandy hopes Sophia (pictured at one-and-a-half-years-old) will one day walk on her own and talk 

Mandy wants her daughter to be able to walk down the street when she's older without people making comments on her appearance  

Mandy's biggest wish for Sophia is that one day she will walk and talk, however she has not let herself think past that.

'We’ll never underestimate her but we haven’t allowed ourselves to think that far ahead,' she said. 

'If she could walk that would be major and feed herself.

'I know they’re only basic things for some but for us that’s huge.'
