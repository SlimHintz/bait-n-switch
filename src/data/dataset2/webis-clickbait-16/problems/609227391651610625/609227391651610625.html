If Crystal Pepsi's back, we want these '90s things, too

By Lisa Respers France, CNN

Updated 1702 GMT (0002 HKT) June 11, 2015

Story highlights

  • Crystal Pepsi is reportedly returning
  • We'd like to see Furbys, the Spice Girls and old-school Nicolas Cage

(CNN)Crystal Pepsi -- the clear soda that made waves in 1992-93 -- is set to make a return to store shelves, and since companies are reviving so many things from the '90s, we want in on the action.

Look at this can of Crystal Pepsi and tell us you don't have Van Halen's "Right Now" in your head.

What with "The X-Files" coming back and Cartoon Network releasing the photo of its live-action "Powerpuff Girls" reboot, the time seems ripe for bringing back some other cool things from the 1990s.

Things like:

The Spice Girls

Sorry, but girl group Fifth Harmony is just not enough for us. We want the original sassy Brit girl group back and making music. A musical based on their songs closed in 2013, and fans have been clamoring for years for a reunion. Make it happen, Posh Spice!

    'Buffy the Vampire Slayer'

    This one is such a no-brainer, it barely needs an explanation. Creator Joss Whedon never needed to do anything else after gifting the world with Sarah Michelle Gellar kicking tail as Buffy on television for seven seasons.

    '90s Nicolas Cage

    "Honeymoon in Vegas," "Face/Off," "Con Air" and "Leaving Las Vegas"? You have to admit that Cage in the 1990s was much better than later Cage.

    Nicolas Cage in "Leaving Las Vegas."

    The music

    Everybody from N' Sync to Lisa Loeb, R.E.M., Ice Cube and Nirvana. There was some good music during that decade. Heck, we even welcome '90s Fiona Apple with open arms -- but only '90s Fiona.

    Original Furbys

    Adorable. The cuddly fur robots -- which have been revamped over the years and now have smartphone apps -- were slightly creepy but still cute.

    Furbies were popular toys in the 1990s.

    Dunkaroos

    Remember those cinnamon graham crackers with the multicolored frosting? They were the bomb! We aren't sure if Betty Crocker is still selling this snack anywhere, as they have been hard to find since being discontinued in the U.S. around 2012.
