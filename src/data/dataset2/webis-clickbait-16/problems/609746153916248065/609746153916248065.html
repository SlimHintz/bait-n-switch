17 gorgeous planters to help your garden glow

1.4k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Andrea Romano2015-06-13 14:30:04 UTC

You've picked out the perfect plants, now it's time to make yourself the perfect planter.

Adding some greenery to your home not only has a ton of health benefits, it is also a pleasing aesthetic addition to any room. But beauty shouldn't stop just at your flowers and ferns.

See also: 15 edible plants you can grow in your tiny apartment

You can turn any planter — whether it's a classic terra cotta pots or simple, empty soup cans — into one-of-a-kind, DIY pieces that fit your style perfectly.

DIY Planters

  1. 1. Gold leaf planter

    Add a bit of glitz without losing all the earthy appeal of your terracotta planters by adding gold leaf foil to the surface.

    Image: Brit + Co

  2. 2. Himmeli hanging planter

    If your perfect planter just needs a little dressing up, try a chic and minimal himmeli design to keep your plant high and dry.

    Image: @ashandcrafts on Instagram

  3. 3. Colored LED planter

    Micro LED lights are a fun and inventive accent for your plants. Plus they look great indoors and outdoors.

    Image: Brit + Co

  4. 4. Neon colorblock planter

    Add a bright burst of color to your decor. Pinks, yellows and oranges will really make your lush greenery stand out.

    Image: Lexy Ward Proper

  5. 5. Woven hanging planters

    If you love the bohemian look, dress up some upcycled planters with a gorgeous woven design concocted entirely by you.

    Image: Francesca Stone Fall For DIY

  6. 6. Faux copper planter

    Cover a simple cannister with a copper facade, and voila! You have a minimal desk plant that anyone can envy.

    Image: Caroline Burke Burkatron

  7. 7. Diamond hanging planter

    Construct a geometric diamond shape to keep your plants nestled high above.

    Image: @craftedsparrow on Instagram

  8. 8. Balsa wood planter

    Earthy, wood accents and plants have always gone hand-in-hand. Cover your mini desk plant in pretty, soft balsa wood.

    Image: Francesca Stone Fall For DIY

  9. 9. Woven mini-planter

    Keep your plants nice and cozy with a woven cover.

    Image: @fallfordiy on Instagram

  10. 10. Faux marble planter

    Since real marble can get heavy and expensive, try out a faux look that is just as gorgeous and way cheaper.

    Image: Francesca Stone Fall For DIY

  11. 11. Colander hanging planter

    Some plants require a little aeration to stay healthy, and colanders are a fun, kitschy way of fulfilling your plant's needs while giving you the right aesthetic.

    Image: Kayln Brooke Creative Savings

  12. 12. Macrame hanging planter

    Grab some soft, jersey fabric yarn (or better yet, make your own) to hang your planters with bright and colorful macrame.

    Image: Brit + Co

  13. 13. Copper PVC wall planter

    PVC fittings are inexpensive and you can find them at just about any hardware store. Make them shine with some copper paint you've got a one-of-a-kind wall piece.

    Image: Shellie Wilson Craft Bits

  14. 14. Floppy disk planters

    Still have old floppy disks floating around your office? Turn them into something useful that is also good for the earth.

    Image: Brit + Co

  15. 15. Concrete planter

    Mixing small amounts of concrete is easier than you think. Plus, you can form it into interesting geometric shapes that perfectly fit your plants.

    Image: Caroline Burke Burkatron

  16. 16. Fabric-covered planter

    Soften up your plant's look with some pretty fabric.

    Image: Caroline Burke Burkatron

  17. 17. Hanging egg planter

    If you're going au naturel this gardening season, reuse some of those eggs from breakfast for your tiny plants.

    Image: Francesca Stone Fall For DIY
