Where ‘Orange Is the New Black’ Left Off: A Season 3 Primer

  • Article
  • Comments (3)

  • Feature

  • Kate Mulgrew

  • Laura Prepon

  • Natasha Lyonne

  • Netflix

  • Orange is the New Black

  • Taryn Manning

  • Taylor Schilling

  • Uzo Aduba

  • By
  • Sarene Leeds
Netflix

The third season of “Orange Is the New Black” is about to hit us like Miss Rosa behind the wheel of a prison van – hard and fast. With 13 brand-new episodes set to arrive on Netflix all at once this Friday, viewers need to be caught up and on their game before we reunite with the lethal (well, just some of them) ladies of Litchfield Penitentiary. Since most of us probably haven’t thought about Season 2 since we binge-watched it a year ago, here’s a refresher on what went down – and where things stand – with our favorite inmates and prison employees.

Piper Chapman (Taylor Schilling)

Taylor Schilling as Piper in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jojo Whilden/Netflix

Much like Andy Dufresne in “The Shawshank Redemption” (“I had to come to prison to be a crook”), Piper has grown more devious the longer she’s remained behind bars. She spent all of Season 2 incarcerated while her ex-girlfriend, Alex, who got Piper mixed up in the drug game, is granted an early release after she reneged on their deal to lie on the stand. Piper’s duplicity reached its pinnacle at the end of the season when, in a twisted combination of vengeance and lust, she had her best friend, Polly (Maria Dizzia) – who conveniently had begun dating Piper’s ex-fiancé, Larry (Jason Biggs, who will not appear in Season 3) – call Alex’s probation officer and tell him Alex was about to skip town.

Alex Vause (Laura Prepon)

Laura Prepon as Alex in “Orange Is the New Black”
Ali Goldstein/Netflix

The best thing to ever happen to a pair of librarian glasses, Piper’s former drug-smuggling girlfriend Alex Vause didn’t appear much in Season 2. But now, thanks to Piper’s sneaky machinations, Prepon’s return to series-regular status and this trailer, rest assured that there will be plenty of sexual drama between these onetime lovers in Season 3.

Galina “Red” Reznikov (Kate Mulgrew)

Kate Mulgrew as Red in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jessica Miglio/Netflix

Ousted prison chef/matriarch Red had a rough go of things in Season 2. She lost much of her clout within the Litchfield hierarchy, and with the return of her old smuggling rival, the cunning Yvonne “Vee” Parker (Lorraine Toussaint), Red’s Season 1 starve-out of Piper seemed like child’s play. Vee and Red’s power struggle came to a head when Red attempted to kill Vee during a thunderstorm, but couldn’t follow through with it. Vee then retaliated in the most vicious way imaginable, sneaking up on Red from behind and “slocking” (a lock stuffed in a sock) her in the face and in the back of her head, landing the Russian mobster in the hospital. But while Red recuperated, she was unaware of a cancer-stricken angel working on her behalf: In the final minutes of Season 2, Vee, having broken out of Litchfield, was run down at top speed by veteran bank robber Miss Rosa (Barbara Rosenblat), who staged an escape of her own upon learning her chemotherapy treatments were no longer working.

While we don’t know the fate of Miss Rosa, who plowed into Litchfield’s resident villain to the tune of Blue Oyster Cult’s “Don’t Fear the Reaper,” Vee’s lifeless face strongly suggests that Rosa succeeded where Red could not. Here’s hoping this means the Russian empire will strike back in Season 3.

Sam Healy (Michael J. Harney)

Michael J. Harney as Mr. Healy in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jessica Miglio/Netflix

Season 2 further explored the prison counselor’s loveless marriage to a Ukrainian mail-order bride and showcased his woeful ineptitude at forging respectful relationships with the Litchfield inmates (except for Pennsatucky). His attempt to start a counseling group, Safe Place, was admirable – he even brought Ukrainian cookies! – but due to his tendency to play favorites (most of the ladies weren’t too keen on his decision to give the WASPy Piper furlough) and his virulent and vocal disgust of lesbians, no one showed up after the first session. Still, Healy demonstrated he wasn’t without a heart at the end of the season, when he forged a work order to prove to investigators that Crazy Eyes – whom, thanks to Vee, they already had pegged as their primary suspect – was not behind Red’s slocking.

Suzanne “Crazy Eyes” Warren (Uzo Aduba)

Uzo Aduba as Crazy Eyes in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jessica Miglio/Netflix

While the reason for Crazy Eyes’s incarceration remains a mystery, we got some great backstory on Uzo Aduba’s Emmy Award-winning character in Season 2. Although flashbacks show young Suzanne was raised by nurturing adoptive parents, she was made to feel like an outsider by her predominantly white suburban community. Her fragile state made her the ideal target for Vee’s form of pure, manipulative evil: Through charm and masterful mothering, Vee turned Crazy Eyes into her one-woman army, using Suzanne’s violent tendencies to instill fear throughout the entire prison community. But after Vee slocked Red, she needed someone to take the fall for her – and volunteered Crazy Eyes for the job by convincing the unstable felon to confess to the crime. Suzanne was ultimately spared when her fellow inmates told the investigators that Vee was behind the attack on Red, and Sam Healy forged a work order that proved her innocence. Tragically, if this Season 3 first look teaser is any indication, Crazy Eyes still remains ignorant to Vee’s true nature – as well as her living status.

Tasha “Taystee” Jefferson (Danielle Brooks)

Danielle Brooks as Taystee in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jojo Whilden/Netflix

We knew from Taystee’s short-lived stint on the outside in Season 1 that the wisecracking, book-smart (she’s an “Outlander” fan!) Litchfield inmate was alone in the world, but the touching Season 2 flashbacks to her childhood filled in a lot of the blanks. She grew up an orphan, and the closest she ever got to being adopted came in the form of her unofficial mother figure, Vee, who took a shine to the sassy little girl and eventually brought her into her drug-dealing business. When Vee lands back at Litchfield, Taystee’s dependence on her manifests itself once again, especially in the way Vee is able to drive a wedge between Taystee and her BFF Poussey (Samira Wiley) almost immediately. The two besties spend most of the season at odds – Poussey’s unrequited love for her straight friend also makes things a bit awkward for a time – but once Vee’s schemes are exposed, their friendship is renewed and they close out Season 2 stronger than ever.

Tiffany “Pennsatucky” Doggett (Taryn Manning)

Taryn Manning as Pennsatucky in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jessica Miglio/Netflix

The character who did the most significant 180° shift in Season 2, Pennsatucky pretty much dropped her evangelical Christian faith-healing persona and left Crazy Eyes (who amusingly called her “Pennsabama” in one episode) to fill the void of frightening, brutal inmate. Having survived her savage beatdown from Piper at the end of Season 1, the nearly toothless meth head got herself a gorgeous new set of choppers courtesy of the U.S. government and started up a weird friendship with Sam Healy, who served as her anger management counselor. Together, they began the support group Safe Place, which unfortunately didn’t go any further than one meeting. Although Pennsatucky chilled out on the violence in Season 2, her homophobia and all-around bigotry – exacerbated by the equally ignorant Healy – left a lot to be desired.

Dayanara Diaz (Dascha Polanco)

Dascha Polanco as Daya in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jessica Miglio/Netflix

She’s still pregnant and corrections officer John Bennett (Matt McGorry) is still the father, but their future together – and the future of their unborn child remains a hot mess. When CO George “Pornstache” Mendez (Pablo Schreiber) returned to work at Litchfield following his Season 1 suspension (Daya pretended to seduce Mendez so they would be caught and she could pin the paternity on him), Daya once and for all named Pornstache as the father of her baby. Even though a guilt-ridden Bennett confessed the truth to his supervisor, Joe Caputo, Season 2 ends with Caputo advising the CO to keep his mouth shut – and Pornstache somewhere out there celebrating his impending daddy status.

Joe Caputo (Nick Sandow)

Nick Sandow as Caputo in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jojo Whilden/Netflix

Thanks to Piper’s in-house investigative work, the Executive Assistant to the Warden, Natalie Figueroa (Alysia Reiner), is out of a job for embezzling prison funds at the end of Season 2, and Joe Caputo is in. But really, the only thing anyone cared about with regards to Caputo is the fact that he plays in a local band called Side Boob. And that it provided Litchfield’s electrical-shop manager Joel Luschek (Matt Peters) with the best line of the season: “You look like the gay Edge.”

Nicole “Nicky” Nichols (Natasha Lyonne)

Natasha Lyonne as Nicky in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jessica Miglio/Netflix

Other than her hilarious sex contest with Big Boo (Lea DeLaria), Nicky’s story lines were pretty subdued in Season 2 – up until the finale, “We Have Manners. We’re Polite.”  Justice is absolutely served when Nicky steals and hides Vee’s stash of heroin as revenge for the slocking attack on Red (whom Nicky deems her surrogate mother). The loss of her precious product causes Vee to panic, flee Litchfield and end up as roadkill. But as grateful as we are to Nicky for acting as the catalyst for Vee’s demise, it might have come at a heavy price: Nicky is a recovering heroin addict, and the way she gazes longingly at Vee’s loot suggests that she’s probably not going to just flush it down the toilet.

Lorna Morello (Yael Stone)

Yael Stone as Lorna in “Orange Is the New Black”
Jessica Miglio/Netflix

In a prison filled with toughened mobsters and drug dealers, who would’ve thought that the perky, crimson-lipped van driver would end up the one with the most frightening backstory? Lorna Morello’s sweet, wedding-planning demeanor was shattered in Season 2 when flashbacks revealed that her adored fiancé, Christopher (“Christ-eh-fah”), was in fact a man she had been stalking after one failed date. Courtroom scenes further disclosed that Morello had attempted to kill Christopher and his actual fiancée by planting an explosive under their car. Her story also goes to show that in some cases, even locking someone up in prison doesn’t keep the victims entirely safe from a dangerous obsessive: After driving Miss Rosa to her chemo session, Morello steals the van and breaks into Christopher’s house to take a bath while wearing his fiancée’s veil. And speaking of Miss Rosa and her own prison-van caper – it’s Morello who ends up handing over the car keys to the dying inmate, enabling both her escape and her final moments as a free woman.
