'GMA' had a bad Monday and tweeted a GIF of dead Mufasa

1.2k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Tricia Gilbride2015-06-09 15:58:18 UTC

Don't you just hate it when your power-hungry brother pushes you off a cliff and you're trampled by a stampede of wildebeest? Typical Monday.

That probably wasn't exactly what was going through the mind of the Good Morning America employee who tweeted a depressing Lion King GIF of Simba trying to wake Mufasa's corpse with the caption: "RT if this was you this morning... #Mondays" this week.

See also: 8 dark film directors paired with their perfect Disney movie

The Verge screencapped the unfortunate post before it was taken down two hours and at least 98 retweets later. A lot of people are tweeting from beyond the grave, apparently.

It's a good rule of thumb for social media managers to have some sort of knowledge of beloved children's movies before they mine them for content. Almost every Disney movie is rife with tragedy to prepare children for the cruel world they can look forward to inhabiting for the rest of their lives, so it's important to be discerning when incorporating them in your brand.

There's also simply no need get creative when posting cartoon cat GIFs on social media to express how you feel about Mondays. Garfield exists for a reason.
