    ‘Fun Home’ won Best Musical at the Tony Awards. Alison Bechdel, center, wrote the graphic novel on which the musical is based.
    Getty Images

    The 2015 Tony Awards show promises a good deal of drama thanks to strong competition in a number of categories including the glittering prize of best new musical (can “Fun Home” possibly edge out “An American in Paris?”).

    More In Tony Awards

    • Tony Awards 2015: See the Complete Winners List

    • Tony Awards 2015: Inside the Dress Rehearsal

    Shows that win for best new musical typically enjoy longer runs and improved profit, so nabbing the award is a big deal. While there are many other prizes given out during the Tonys, none carry as much commercial weight.

    Another close one will be Best Actress in a Musical, largely seen as a tie between Kelli O’Hara of “The King and I” and Kristin Chenoweth of “On the 20th Century.” (Chenoweth will also co-host tonight’s show, with Alan Cumming.)

    Helen Mirren attends the 2015 Tony Awards at Radio City Music Hall on June 7, 2015 in New York City.
    Getty Images

    The awards-show broadcast is seen as an opportunity for Broadway to capture TV viewers’ attention and convince them to come out and see a stage production.
