An American Colonial Home in Australia

June 7, 2015 11:55 p.m. ET 0 COMMENTS

fullscreen
This colonial New England-inspired home in Australia has verdant gardens, large orchards and striking river views. It sits on an expansive 4.42-hectare (10.9-acre) estate in Brisbane’s western suburbs. American owner Terry Peabody, the chairman of wine label Craggy Range, purchased the three-level main house in 1969. Over the next 30 years he purchased three additional titles, each with a house, which he demolished. He purchased a fifth title with a manager’s residence for the estate’s staff. Saul Goodwin …
Mr. Peabody expanded the home by adding a large master bedroom featuring American oak fixtures, limestone flooring and an en suite. He also added two more bedrooms and a nursery, as well as a kitchen, dining and living wing. Other additions include a swimming pool and entertainment areas, such as the billiard room and sauna. ‘And then we added a whole new summerhouse, which is on the far end of the property, for entertaining,’ Mr. Peabody says. ‘That was about five years ago.’ The main house is 9,192 square feet and the summerhouse is 2,788 square feet. Saul Goodwin …
Mr. Peabody says he has always liked the New England style because ‘it is incredibly charming.’ This house was built in the 1950s. Saul Goodwin …
The large master bedroom is shown here. It has an open fireplace and an enclosed balcony with views of the garden and Brisbane River. The home has 10 bedrooms and eight bathrooms (including those in the manager’s residence). Saul Goodwin …
The property has a swimming pool, a tennis court, and a variety of formal and informal living areas including a music room, a home office, a cellar and a bar. Saul Goodwin …
‘All of the bedrooms in the main house have spectacular river views, and also views of the garden,’ Mr. Peabody says. ‘It gives a feeling of living in the country and still only being half an hour from the city.’ The home is in the residential suburb of Moggill, which has numerous other homes on large acreages, too. Saul Goodwin …
The kitchen, shown here, has granite countertops, timber cabinetry and Gaggenau appliances. Saul Goodwin …
The owner says the glass conservatory-style lounge, shown here, is another ‘entertainment area and has a glorious view of the river.’ There also are manicured gardens with mature magnolia trees, white roses and varieties of eucalypts, as well as an olive grove, an apple orchard and a citrus orchard, which includes oranges, grapefruits and kaffir limes. Saul Goodwin …
When the owner built the deck, shown here, 15 years ago, he wanted to save the existing Poinciana trees (native to Madagascar) in the space. ‘So we built it around them,’ Mr. Peabody says. ‘It is also a lovely place to entertain and dine.’ Saul Goodwin …
The summerhouse includes a wide sandstone terrace, a kitchen and a large dining hall. The French antique table, shown here, was purchased from a store in Melbourne. Mr. Peabody says the property is a lovely place to raise a family. He is selling it because he has bought another home in Brisbane. Saul Goodwin …
The home was first put on the market in September 2014 but was taken off when the owner went on holiday. It was relisted in March for offers in excess of 12 million Australian dollars (US$9.2 million) with Unique Estates agent Nicolette van Wijngaarden. The furnishings aren’t included in the sale price but are negotiable. Saul Goodwin …
