Arrested prison employee allegedly gave hacksaw blades to escaped killers

739
Shares
Share

What's This?
By Brittany Levine2015-06-12 22:20:40 UTC

UPDATED 11:50 p.m. ET

New York State Police arrested a prison instructor on Friday in connection to an elaborate prison breakout by two convicted killers that has widely been compared to the movie Shawshank Redemption.

Joyce Mitchell, 51, is suspected of befriending the men and providing them with tools, such as hacksaw blades, chisels, a screwdriver bit and a punch to help them escape the maximum-security Clinton Correctional Facility in upstate New York, according to court documents. The men crawled through a manhole outside prison walls on Saturday. Authorities believe Mitchell had planned to drive the getaway car for the inmates, but instead decided against it.

See also: Prison employee fingered in 'Shawshank' escape plot

Mitchell, a sewing instructor in the prison's tailor shop, has been charged with promoting prison contraband in the first degree and criminal facilitation in the fourth degree, according to a statement from New York State Police.

At her arraignment late Friday evening in Plattsburgh, New York, Mitchell pled not guilty. Her bail was set at $100,000 cash or $200,000 bond for the felony charge of promoting prison contraband and $10,000 cash or $20,000 bond for the misdemeanor charge of criminal facilitation. She was remanded into custody until Monday morning.

The maximum sentence for providing prisoners with contraband is seven years in prison, District Attorney Andrew Wylie said at a press conference Friday evening. The maximum sentence for criminal facilitation is one year in county jail.

"Any contraband introduced into the facility is critical," Major Charles Guess of the New York State Police said at the press conference, adding that interviews with Mitchell have aided the massive manhunt for the inmates, Richard Matt and David Sweat.

"Our interviews with Joyce Mitchell have been fruitful," Guess said.

Mitchell's husband is not in custody and has not been charged with a crime, authorities said.

More than 800 state, federal and local law enforcement officers are searching for the men, Guess said, noting that there is no conclusive evidence they have left the area around Dannemora, New York. Residents had reported seeing two men jumping a stone wall in a nearby rural area.

"We have every resource on the ground at our disposal," Guess said, noting that wet weather could make the escapees more desperate. They are "cold, wet, tired and hungry," he said. Authorities do not know if the two men have stuck together or separated, but search teams are prepared for either scenario, Guess said.

The murderers had dug their way out of prison by cutting a steel wall and making their way through a series of tunnels, finally climbing out of a manhole outside prison walls. In the 1994 movie Shawshank Redemption, prisoners crawl through sewer tunnels to get out.

Mitchell, of Dickinson Center, New York may have had an improper relationship with Sweat, authorities have said.

Over the past year, officials looked into whether she had ties to the 34-year-old Sweat, who was serving a life sentence for killing a sheriff's deputy, Wylie told The Associated Press.

The investigation didn't turn up anything solid enough to warrant disciplinary charges against her, the district attorney said.

"But action, I think, was taken to separate the two of them for a period," he said.

Matt, 48, was convicted of torturing, kidnapping, dismembering and killing his former boss in 1997. The victim was 76.

Officials have not publicly specified what items Mitchell, who has worked at the prison for at least five years, may have supplied to the prisoners. Wylie said, however, she did not give the inmates the power tools they used to make their escape.

Additional reporting by The Associated Press.
