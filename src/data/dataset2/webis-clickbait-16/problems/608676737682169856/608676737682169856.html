Government to sell 15pc of Royal Mail overnight

Department for Business, Innovation and Skills authorises sale of remaining 30pc shareholding, with half expected to be sold in an accelerated placing

Royal Mail was privatised in October 2013 Photo: Getty Images

By John Ficenec

9:00PM BST 10 Jun 2015

Follow

Comments

The Government has announced plans to sell a 15pc stake in Royal Mail to take advantage of "current market conditions", as it seeks to complete the controversial privatisation.

The placing of 150m shares will take place overnight through a process known as an "accelerated book build" to institutional investors only with retail investors unable to access the process.

The deal will raise more than £770m at current market prices and reduce the Government stake from 30pc, to 15pc.

George Osborne said in his Mansion House speech on Wednesday night: "We want to help the Royal Mail attract more investment and serve its customers, and use the money we raise in return to pay down the national debt.

"And we’re also going to make sure that there is a special bonus for the workforce who have done such a great job turning Royal Mail around.

"Thanks to them, Royal Mail’s share price has risen; so we’re going to give more of the shares to the staff.

"Tomorrow [Thursday] we will announce more details of that, and how much of our stake we have sold.”

The Chancellor revealed last week that the Treasury is looking to raise a total of £1.5bn, as part of a £4.5bn debt reduction plan.

The Government said it had appointed bankers at Rothschild to advise Business Secretary Sajid Javid on selling the stake, instead of Lazard, which advised on the 2013 share sale.

Lazard's role in the initial public offering sparked controversy when it emerged that its asset management division was one of the priority investors that had bought shares at an early stage before selling them at an £8m profit on behalf of its clients.

Bank of America Merrill Lynch, Goldman Sachs and JP Morgan Cazenove have been appointed to act as Joint Bookrunners in connection with the placing.

• George Osborne announces Royal Mail sell off and deep spending cuts

The 499-year-old postal service was privatised in 2013, but the 38pc rise in its share price on its first day on the stock market led to accusations that the Government had sold it off too cheaply.

Royal Mail was floated at 330p a share, and stood at 516p on Wednesday, valuing the Treasury's remaining holding at £1.5bn.

Details of the share price for the placing and the number of shares will be announced at a later date.

A Department for Business spokesman said: "The Business Secretary has today authorised a sale process to begin. Current market conditions represent a good opportunity to achieve value for the taxpayer.

"The universal service is strongly protected by law and Ofcom has a duty to ensure its provision. Therefore the Government sees no policy reason to retain a stake in Royal Mail."
