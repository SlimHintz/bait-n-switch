Alton Towers denies shutting more rides over fallen clip

From the topic Life

11/06/15

Alton Towers has denied reports that a large clip fell off a ride on Thursday, leading to more closures at the theme park.

Three rollercoasters are already shut after five people were seriously injured on the Smiler ride last week.

But the park's told Newsbeat rumours that the Runaway Mine Train and Air were also shut are exaggerated.

It says both were affected by "standard minor delays".

A spokeswoman also claims a metal clip, photographed by a visitor at the park, "had not come off any ride and is not part of a ride".

In a statement Alton Towers says: "Air was closed for a short time this morning to allow an extra shuttle to be added.

"The Runaway Mine Train opened late due to a late sign-off from the technical team responsible for pre-opening checks."

Image caption Five people were seriously hurt in the Smiler crash

It says both are now fully working and adds: "It is natural that given the current circumstances, our guests (and the media) are more focused on any minor delay or technical stoppage.

"But all of the rides undergo comprehensive pre-opening checks and any delays that we are experiencing now are within our expected level.

"Alton Towers puts the health and safety of its guests at the forefront of everything it does, which means that from time to time rides will be closed to allow for technical issues to be reviewed.

"We will not operate a ride if there is any suggestion of a safety compromise."

Daniel Thorpe, who's been treated for a collapsed lung and a fractured leg after the Smiler crash, has thanked medical teams and the public for their support which he says has been a "great comfort".

In a statement he says: "I'm now looking forward to getting better, getting home and getting back to normal."

He was in the front row with 20-year-old Vicky Balch, who is in a serious but stable condition.

On Wednesday, the brother of 17-year-old Leah Washington, whose leg had to be amputated after the crash, said she had stood up for the first time.

Luke Washington said her condition was rapidly improving every day after she was kept on a life support machine at first.

Image caption Leah Washington is being treated at Royal Stoke Hospital

"Compared to last week, when she got to hospital and I saw her for the first time, it's a world of difference", he said.

Leah's boyfriend Joe Pugh's being treated in the same hospital for broken knees and serious hand injuries. The couple had also been sitting in the front row.

Chanda Chauhan who was in the second row suffered internal injuries.

Alton Towers reopened on Monday after shutting for five days because of the crash.

The Smiler ride is still closed while investigators look into what happened. Several other rides also closed as a precaution.

It's the first serious accident in the park's history and it owners say everyone injured will get compensation.

One Direction has sent a personal video message to Leah Washington, who is described as a "total fangirl" of the band by friends.

She's also been invited to meet them backstage on their UK tour as soon as she's well enough.
