Two Aussies play horrible prank on their friend, telling him he killed a man

805
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Jenni RyallAustralia2015-06-11 04:21:15 UTC

Too far?

A couple of serial pranksters have crossed an invisible line when it comes to over-the-top YouTube practical jokes.

Australian guys Mark Reynolds and Richy Sandham, who go by the name The Royal Stampede, asked their mate Michael to help them with their latest video. Michael is dressed in a rabbit suit and told to jump out at passersby on the streets of Adelaide.

See also: Dog wants new owner because current one doesn't know when to end a prank

Unbeknownst to Michael, the prank is actually on him. As he surprises two random strangers in the street — a woman and her dad — the man feigns chest pains and drops to the ground.

His daughter screams he has a heart condition, as Michael can be heard apologising over and over. As the man is rushed to hospital, Michael becomes more and more distressed. As the camera continues to roll, he is then told by his mates the dad is dead. What started as a fun bunny rabbit prank has suddenly turned to tragedy. Michael freaks out and breaks down.

In the next minute, the prank gets into next level territory: The man walks out of the hospital — very much alive.

Sandham confirmed to Mashable Australia Michael is definitely not in on the prank, and said he has forgiven them for making him cry. "Michael was annoyed but is now over it, we are still friends," he wrote in an email. "I think we pushed the boundaries you have to to get noticed these days."

The moral of the story: pranks can kill.
