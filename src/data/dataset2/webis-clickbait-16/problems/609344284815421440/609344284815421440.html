Snoop Dogg and all the other candidates for Twitter CEO

1.0k
Shares

What's This?
By Seth Fiegerman2015-06-11 23:42:48 UTC

Who will be the next CEO of one of the world's most influential social networks?

Twitter shocked many on Thursday by announcing that CEO Dick Costolo is stepping down on July 1 and will be replaced by Jack Dorsey, one of Twitter's cofounders and its first CEO from the early days. Dorsey is interim CEO — without pay — while a three-member committee and Twitter's board searches for a permanent replacement.

On a conference call with analysts after the announcement, Dorsey offered one clue into his key criteria for the next CEO of Twitter: "We're looking for someone who uses and loves the product in every single way."

That means the next CEO will have to be an active tweeter. With that in mind, here are a few possible candidates, ranked by their Twitter metrics as well as their qualifications.

Adam Bain

Hiring pitch: Grew Twitter's advertising business into a juggernaut that generated more than $400 million in the fourth quarter.

Relevant experience: Worked at News Corp. and the Los Angeles Times, giving him media experience — a key focus for Twitter.

Follower count: 68,200

Tweet count: 7,392

Anthony Noto

Hiring pitch: Noto is already in Twitter's C-Suite, working as CFO. He has stepped up as Twitter's voice to Wall Street in lieu of Costolo and recently took charge of Twitter's marketing department. (One downside: he's not a product person and sometimes makes rookie mistakes using Twitter.)

Relevant experience: He helped take Twitter public while at Goldman Sachs.

Follower count: 18,800

Tweet count: 667

Katie Jacobs Stanton

Hiring pitch: She oversees Twitter's news and television efforts, which will only become more important in the future. Having a female CEO might also give Twitter greater credibility as it tackles issues of abuse and harassment on the platform.

Relevant experience: She previously held positions at Google, Yahoo and the White House.

Follower count: 95,000

Tweet count: 20,000

Kevin Weil

Hiring pitch: He is now in charge of all product development and design at Twitter and has presided over the company's fastest product rollout cycle ever. (Then again, maybe you want to keep him in that role so he continues doing that.)

Relevant experience: He is the embodiment of Dorsey's description of someone "uses and loves the product in every single way."

Follower count: 50,800

Tweet count: 14,900

Mike McCue

Hiring pitch: He built up Flipboard into a nearly billion-dollar company and has good relationships with publishers and the overall technology industry.

Relevant experience: He served on Twitter's board of directors.

Follower count: 17,700

Tweet count: 7,470

Jack Dorsey

Hiring pitch: He is widely credited with coming up with the original idea for Twitter and is the only one other than cofounder Ev Williams who can say he has previous experience serving as Twitter CEO. That said, he was later criticized for being an ineffective and scattered leader at Twitter.

Relevant experience: He is currently the CEO of Square, a popular mobile payments startup, and has balanced that with being Twitter's chairman of the board.

Follower count: 3 million

Tweet count: 16,700

Snoop Dogg

Hiring pitch: Proven marketer and innovator. Has smoked up with Twitter team. And he wants the gig.

Relevant experience: He's Snoop Dogg.

Follower count: 12.3 million

Tweet count: 29,700
