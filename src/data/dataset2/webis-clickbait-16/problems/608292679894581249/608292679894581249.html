Jurassic World: by numbers

Everything you need to know about Jurassic World, the latest instalment in the Jurassic Park franchise, which hits UK cinemas this week

By Geraldine Cooper and Joe Brennan

7:49AM BST 11 Jun 2015

Comments

Hard as it may be for some of us to believe, it is more than two decades since Steven Spielberg's blockbuster Jurassic Park first roared into cinemas.

With its brand new special effects and cutting-edge animatronics, Jurassic Park broke new ground and thrilled audiences around the world.

Chris Pratt, then just 13, was one of the first people to watch the film when it was released in 1993.

Little could he have imagined that he would one day star in its spin-off, Jurassic World.

"I saw it at the theatre, my first big event movie and to be here now, to be part of this, to be part of this big event, it's very meaningful," said Pratt, who plays park ranger Owen in Jurassic World.

Jurassic World, a reboot of the original, is directed by Colin Trevorrow, but Spielberg was close at hand throughout the making of the film, acting as its executive producer.

• Bryce Dallas Howard on why Jurassic World isn't sexist

"He was watching the dailies every day – so he would see something I'd done and he'd say 'You know what would be great is what if you had a shot where this happened?' and he would draw it out and he would send it to me and I would go shoot it," Trevorrow told The Associated Press.

Spielberg first mooted the idea of Jurassic World 14 years ago, following the huge success of 1997's The Lost World and Jurassic Park 3 in 2001.

Jurassic World: everything you need to know

The first three films took in excess of $2.19 billion at the box office worldwide.

With an estimated budget of $150 million, Jurassic World will be looking to capitalise on the success of its predecessors.
