Dozens of hot air balloons filling London skies look like straight from a postcard

3.3k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Blathnaid HealyUK2015-06-08 09:45:26 UTC

LONDON — Very early risers were in for a treat on Sunday morning as 40 hot air balloons ascended, filling the sky over London shortly after sunrise.

See also: 7 London tube maps that might be better than the new official one

The Lord Mayor's Hot Air Balloon Regatta, organised by the City of London, is the first time in more than 20 years that there has been a mass ascent of hot air balloons over the City, with the event taking place for just over an hour. The 100-foot balloons took off from Shoreditch Park in Hackney, moving across the Thames between Tower Bridge, the Shard and Canary Wharf before concluding in Gravesend. The last two mass balloon ascents happened over the City of London in 1987 and 1993.

Exclusive Ballooning, which operated the regatta, got permission fro the National Air Traffic Services and the Civil Aviation Authority for the early-morning event, which raised £78,884 ($120,146) for City of London Lord Mayor Alan Yarrow's Lord Mayor's Appeal.
