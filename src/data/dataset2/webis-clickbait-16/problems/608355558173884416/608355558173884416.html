A year after cancer diagnosis, Texans OL David Quessenberry eyes return to field

By Sam Cooper June 9, 2015 1:50 PM Shutdown Corner
  • 
  • 
  • 
  • 
Content preferences

    Houston Texans' David Quessenberry (77) who is battling cancer is acknowledged by teammates before an NFL football …

    It’s been only four months since Houston Texas lineman David Quessenberry announced that his cancer was in remission, and he is already eyeing a return to the field.

    Quessenberry was diagnosed with non-Hodgkin’s lymphoma in June 2014. A year later, he’s been back in the weight room with his teammates and is approaching his playing weight of 307 pounds.

    He told reporters Monday that he has no doubt that he’ll play for the Texans again.

    “I will be back out there,” he said, per the Houston Chronicle. “That will be a special day. I’ve been through a lot. That’s something that’s been a vision of mine throughout this whole process.”

    Quessenberry, a sixth-round pick out of San Jose State in 2013, missed his rookie season with a foot injury and was struggling through fatigue and a cough during OTAs last summer. Upon further examination, the cancer was discovered, ending his 2014 season.

    Now, after all that, Quessenberry is working to build his strength back up to what it was a year ago.

    “I come in here, I lift, I work out before the team does in the morning,” he said, per the Texans’ website. “I go to meetings, go out for practice. I’m not cleared to play yet, but I’m on the other field pushing the sleds, running, and doing those things working on getting my strength and stamina back. Then come in here for meetings in the afternoon and taking care of anything else that needs to be done before I get out of here.”

    The team doesn’t have a timetable in place for Quessenberry’s return, but head coach Bill O’Brien said the 2015 season is a possibility.

    “This is a tough guy,” O’Brien said. “This is a guy that went out there and practiced three or four times last year with cancer. Before he was diagnosed, he was fighting through what he thought was probably some type of flu, cold or something.

    "He knows his body really well. He knows where he’s at in his treatments. I have an idea of where he’s at. I would say that this season is a possibility.

    “I would not rule it out, but we haven’t come to that determination yet. He’s here every day. He’s not allowed to be out there as far as in the practice, but he’s out there. You guys see him out there. He’s in all the meetings. He can lift weight. I just think it’s an unbelievable story. It says a lot about him. It says a lot about our organization, I think, that we’ve stuck by him.”

    The one-year anniversary of Quessenberry’s cancer diagnosis came over the weekend. In an Instagram post, he wrote about what his year has been like.

    “A year ago today I had a doctor tell me ‘you have cancer.’ I didn't believe him at first. I thought someone somewhere must have made a mistake. I sat alone in that room feeling sorry for myself... thinking that the life that I had sacrificed so much for was being ripped away from me and there was nothing I could do about it... Fast forward one year later through 10 rounds of chemo, 5 weeks of radiation, countless hours spent in the hospital and lots and lots of ‘bad days.’ I'm back doing what I love with people that I love.

    This picture is of me heading out to practice during these OTAs. I’m not cleared to play yet.. but day by day I'm getting stronger and closer to strapping on the pads and playing the game I love. I woke up today grateful for all that I have. Thank you to everyone who has stood by me this past year. There is no way I could thank you all enough for the prayers and positive thoughts you gave me. The fight and the journey is still not over but I know we have many good days ahead. ‘There is no weapon more powerful than the human soul on fire.’ Every day is a blessing.”
