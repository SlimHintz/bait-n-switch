Why Arsenal need Zlatan Ibrahimovic

The PSG striker might be 33, but the stats show he still has a great deal to offer and he could bring trophies to the Emirates

Could Zlatan Ibrahimovic be the man to end Arsenal' Premier League trophy drought? 

By Alistair Tweedale

12:31PM BST 08 Jun 2015

Follow

Comments

Talk of Zlatan Ibrahimovic making a sensational move to the Premier League has started to become rather predictable. It seems every year that the superstar Swede will be lured to England, with Arsenal reportedly interested this time around.

• Gunners consider £11m bid for Ibrahimovic

But whatever the truth of the story, there's no denying that he might just be the perfect fit for Arsene Wenger, too. At 33 years of age, it is thought that he would also be available at a cut price, yet his scoring record alone surely provides proof that he still has a great deal to offer.


Ibrahimovic has won three Ligue 1 title in three years

He hit 19 goals in 24 Ligue 1 appearances last season, also adding nine in cup competitions and another two in Champions League games.

His total of 30 goals in 35 competitive appearances is better than any Arsenal player managed, with Alexis Sanchez leading the way on 25. Olivier Giroud managed 19, though after that only Aaron Ramsey broke double figures (10), with Danny Welbeck managing just eight in 34 appearances.

• Zlatan Ibrahimovic - the 20 most ridiculous things he has said

Both Sanchez and Giroud were clinical in front of goal, with respective shot conversion rates of 20.3% and 24.1%, but Ibrahimovic fared even better, finishing some 27.1% of the chances presented to him. He clearly is not losing his touch – that is his best such rate in any of the 11 campaigns for which Opta have data.

He also scored more frequently than either of Arsenal’s first choice strikers, netting on average every 105 minutes, compared to every 133 for Giroud and every 184 for Sanchez.

Zlatan also provides impressive creativity, laying on 77 assists in the last 11 league campaigns, and surely surrounded by the attacking talent available at Arsenal he would improve that tally.

It might be suggested that Ligue 1 gives the striker too simple a time of things, but he continues to show his class in Europe. He netted 10 goals in the 2013-14 Champions League campaign – two more than Lionel Messi managed and second only to Cristiano Ronaldo.


Ibrahimovic celebrates scoring for AC Milan against Arsenal in 2012

He also has a poor record against Premier League teams, with just three goals in 16 appearances, but that bicycle kick he scored against England alone proves he can compete against the English.

Besides an unwavering eye for goal, Ibrahimovic would also bring a vast level of experience and an almost unrivalled winning mentality to a club desperate to return to the heights of the years surrounding the turn of the century, when they won the Premier League three times in seven years.


Ibrahimovic scored one of the great goals against England in 2012

Zlatan has played 14 seasons in major European leagues, and has 12 domestic league trophies to his name. In the 12 seasons since his final campaign at Ajax in 2003-04, he has only failed to win the league once (and that year he was only four points off top spot with Milan). In that period he has racked up some 14 major trophies. Arsenal, meanwhile, have won just four.

• Arsenal bank £3m after Barcelona win

The Champions League title continues to evade both club and player, and they will be hopeful that pairing up will give both a fighting chance of changing that. Despite his years, it could be that Ibrahimovic, who as a teenager infamously turned down the chance to trial for Arsene Wenger, could still be the player to turn Arsenal’s fortunes.
